🎯 Last Day Placement Revision Notes

1. Focus on formulas - keep the formula sheet handy
2. Practice Vedic Maths for speed - multiplication, squaring, square roots
3. Master Percentage to Fraction conversions - saves time in exams
4. For Probability, remember: Two dice sum table (7 has max 6 ways)
5. For Blood Relation: draw family tree with + (male) and - (female)
6. For Direction: draw path diagram, mark N-S-E-W clearly
7. For Alligation: (D-M):(M-C) rule saves time in mixture problems
8. For SI/CI: Difference between CI and SI for 2 years = P(R/100)²
9. For Number System: Cyclicity of unit digits (2,4,8,6 | 3,9,7,1 | etc.)
10. Common mistake: Not reading "at least" / "at most" carefully in probability

⚠️ Common Formula Mistakes

❌ Confusing Permutation (order matters) vs Combination (selection)
❌ Forgetting to divide by 100 in percentage calculations
❌ Using SI formula for compound interest problems
❌ Not checking if a number is divisible by 2 before dividing by 6
❌ Assuming "and" means addition (it means multiplication in counting)
❌ Forgetting that circular arrangement = (n-1)! not n!
❌ Not cancelling numerator with denominator in remainder problems

💰 Profit & Loss

CP/SP, Discount, Faulty Weights & Successive Discount

Profit %
P% = (SP - CP)/CP × 100
Loss %
L% = (CP - SP)/CP × 100
SP with Profit
SP = CP × (100 + P%)/100
SP with Loss
SP = CP × (100 - L%)/100
CP from SP (Profit)
CP = SP × 100/(100 + P%)
CP from SP (Loss)
CP = SP × 100/(100 - L%)
Discount %
D% = (MP - SP)/MP × 100
Successive Discount
Net D = a + b - ab/100
Faulty Weights
Profit% = (Error/(True - Error)) × 100

CI Formula

CI - SI (3 years)
Difference = P(R/100)²(3 + R/100)

🔄 Permutation & Combination

Counting Principles, Arrangements, Circular & Geometric

Permutation
nPr = n!/(n-r)! (Order matters)
Combination
nCr = n!/[r!(n-r)!] (Selection)
Fundamental Counting
If ways m and n, total = m × n (AND)
Rule of Addition
If ways m or n, total = m + n (OR)
Circular Arrangement
(n-1)! for n distinct objects in circle
Linear Arrangement
n! ways to arrange n distinct objects
Number of Diagonals
n(n-3)/2 for n-sided polygon
